The Unbridgeable Chasm

The global burden of cancer is intensifying. Yet, between patients and doctors lies a massive information gap built on professional barriers, time constraints, and communication failures.

The Global Crisis: By the Numbers

Cancer is a leading cause of death worldwide. In China alone, new cases reached 4.82 million in 2022—that's 13,200 people diagnosed every single day.

Amidst this severe burden, authoritative and accessible knowledge is critical, yet traditional methods are failing to keep up.

For Patients: Confusion & Anxiety

"Can't understand, Can't remember, Can't use"

Professional Language Barrier

Medical jargon is obscure. Patients struggle to understand their diagnosis and treatment plans, leading to unfocused doctor-patient dialogues.

Lack of Psychological Support

Fear and anxiety are common. Without personalized, understandable knowledge support, patients often feel isolated and helpless.

Outdated Communication

Traditional education relies on static text and lectures. The format is rigid and fails to reach diverse patient populations effectively.

For Doctors: Burnout & Barriers

"Expert knowledge trapped by time and translation"

Overworked & Burnt Out

Heavy clinical and research burdens leave doctors with little time or energy for patient education tasks, pushing science communication to the sidelines.

New Media Skill Gap

Doctors often lack skills in video production or digital marketing, making it hard to translate complex expertise into engaging public content.

Research Translation Blocked

Cutting-edge research remains trapped in academic papers, lacking an effective mechanism to quickly translate findings into public benefit.
